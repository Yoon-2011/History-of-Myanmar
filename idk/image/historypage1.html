Famous Dynasties

  • Pagan Dynasty (849–1297): The "First Burmese Empire." Founded by Anawrahta, it unified the country and built the thousands of temples in Bagan.


  • Toungoo Dynasty (1510–1752): The "Second Burmese Empire." Under King Bayinnaung, it became the largest empire in Southeast Asian history.


  • Konbaung Dynasty (1752–1885): The "Third Burmese Empire." The final dynasty that founded Mandalay, fought wars with China and Siam, and eventually fell to the British.


King Anawrahta

King Anawrahta is arguably the most important figure in Burmese history. He founded the Pagan Empire, the first unified kingdom in the region that would become modern Myanmar. He also unified the Irrawaddy Valley for the first time.

KIng Bayinnaung

King Bayinnaung was known as the "Conqueror of the Ten Directions," he presided over the Toungoo Empire when it was the largest and most powerful empire in the history of Southeast Asia.

King Alaungpaya

King Alaungpaya, known a the unifier A village headman who rose to power, Alaungpaya founded the Konbaung Dynasty (the final dynasty of Burma). He reunited the country after the fall of the Toungoo Dynasty.

King Bodawpaya

Bodaawpaya was a son of Alaungpaya and he is famous for his aggressive expansion and massive construction projects.He conquered the Kingdom of Arakan (Rakhine) in 1784, bringing the famous Mahamuni Buddha image to Mandalay.

King Mindon min

king Mindon Min is remembered as a pious and diplomatic king who tried desperately to modernize his country to save it from British colonial rule.He built Mandalay as the new royal capital in 1857.

Weapons

  • Dha (Sword): The national sword of Burma. Single-edged, gently curved, with a long handle and typically no hand guard
  • Bow and Arrow: The standard long-range weapon for infantry before the 18th century.
  • Infantry Spears: Standard polearms for foot soldiers.
  • Chun-taung: Heavy spears/pikes used specifically by the Elephant Corps.
  • War elephants: were the "armored tanks" of the Burmese military, serving as the ultimate symbol of royal power and the primary shock force in battle.
  • Cavalry: was defined not by heavy armored knights, but by speed, agility, and the use of a specific breed of horse.
  • The King: The absolute ruler and military leader. His right to rule came from his merit and following Buddhist laws.
  • Royal Council (Hluttaw): A group of Ministers who advised the King and managed the daily running of the government.
  • The Monkhood (Sangha): A very respected and tax-free group of Buddhist monks. Monasteries were the main schools and held great moral influence.
  • Crown Service People (A-hmu-dan):They had a lifelong, inherited duty to serve the King (as soldiers, royal artisans, clerks, etc.).They were often given tax-free land for their work.
  • Tax-Paying Villagers (A-thi): They were free farmers and villagers not tied to royal service but they had to pay land taxes to the state.

Major Wars of Burma

  • The Forty Years' War (1385–1424): war between the Kingdom of Ava (Upper Burma) and Kingdom of Hanthawaddy Pegu.
  • Toungoo-Siamese Wars (16th Century): The Toungoo Dynasty (Burma) versus the Ayutthaya Kingdom (Siam, modern Thailand).
  • Konbaung-Siamese Wars (18th Century): The Konbaung Dynasty (Burma) versus the Ayutthaya and later Thonburi/Rattanakosin Kingdoms (Siam).
  • Sino-Burmese War (1765–1769): The Konbaung Dynasty (Burma) versus the Qing Dynasty (China).
  • The Anglo-Burmese Wars (1824–1885): The Konbaung Dynasty (Burma) versus the British East India Company / British Empire.
